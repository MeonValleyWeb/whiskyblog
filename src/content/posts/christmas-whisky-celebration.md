# Christmas whisky celebration (notes)

This wasn’t an “event” write-up — just my notes from a **Christmas-themed tasting**: whiskies that feel right in December, with a few simple snacks that don’t fight the glass.

## The five-dram shape

I like Christmas tastings to follow a gentle arc:

1. **Spiced Highland style** to open (warming but not heavy)
2. **Sherry cask** for fruit cake richness
3. **A little peat** as a winter warmer (smoke as seasoning)
4. **Honey/vanilla** to reset the palate
5. **Port influence** to finish on chocolate and dark fruit

---

## What stood out

- The sherried and port-leaning drams were the stars.
- Gentle peat works brilliantly as a winter warmer and palate reset.
- Food pairings elevated the experience: mince pies with sherry cask, dark chocolate with port influence.

---

## Food pairings that worked

- Mince pies: best with the sherried pour
- Dark chocolate: excellent with port-cask influence
- Smoked salmon: surprisingly good with gentle peat

---

## If you're buying blind

Start with a sherry-cask Christmas dram for classic festive flavours, then add a port-finished bottle if you want dessert richness.
