# At-home Highland tasting

I revisited a few Highland staples this week: Glenmorangie Original, Lasanta, Quinta Ruban and the 18yo. No big event or dinner — just a quiet evening with a couple of friends, a notebook, and some time to compare notes.

## An Evening of Discovery

We started with the **Original 10 year old**, which always earns its place on the shelf. The nose is fresh and sweet — think pears, vanilla and a touch of citrus. On the palate it is gentle and creamy, finishing with light oak spice. It is the bottle I recommend when someone asks for “an easy Highland starter”.

### Sherry and port finishes

The **Lasanta** layers in sherry richness — raisins and cocoa — without losing the house style. The **Quinta Ruban** is where things get interesting: port finishing adds dark fruit and mint chocolate. It is my pick of the flight if you like dessert flavours without heavy sweetness.

### A calm, mature finish

The **18 year old** was the clear highlight. More depth, more silk on the palate, and a finish that hangs on with orange peel and cocoa. It is pricier, but it feels like a treat bottle that is still light on smoke and spice.

---

## What stood out

- The **house style** is elegant and fruity — tall stills doing their job.
- **Cask finishes** add richness without overwhelming the base spirit.
- The **18 year old** showed proper depth and silk — worth the extra spend.

---

## If you're buying blind

Start with the **Original 10 year old** to learn the house style, then jump to **Quinta Ruban** if you want dessert richness.
